About Us

History of JCI Nepal

Junior Chamber International (JCI) Nepal is the national branch of the global JCI organization, which was founded in 1944 in the United States. JCI Nepal was officially established in 1964, making it one of the oldest youth leadership organizations in the country.

Since its inception, JCI Nepal has been dedicated to empowering young people between the ages of 18 and 40 by providing them opportunities for leadership development, community service, and personal growth. Through various local chapters (called Local Organization Members or LOMs) spread across the country, JCI Nepal encourages youth to actively participate in building a better society.

Over the decades, JCI Nepal has played a crucial role in fostering civic responsibility, promoting sustainable development, and nurturing future leaders in Nepal. The organization works in alignment with the global JCI mission to create positive change, with programs that range from leadership training and advocacy to social entrepreneurship and community projects.

Today, JCI Nepal continues to expand its reach and impact, supporting thousands of young Nepalis in becoming proactive citizens who contribute meaningfully to their communities and the nation.

üá≥üáµ Founding of JCI Nepal (Nepal Jaycees)

JCI Nepal, also known as Nepal Jaycees, was officially established in 1964 as the national organization of Junior Chamber International (JCI) in Nepal. The organization was founded with the vision of empowering young people to create positive change through leadership development, community service, and personal growth.

üßë‚Äç‚öñÔ∏è First President: JCI Sen. Dr. Bhekh Bahadur Thapa

The inaugural president of JCI Nepal was JCI Sen. Dr. Bhekh Bahadur Thapa, a distinguished diplomat and public servant. Dr. Thapa's leadership laid the foundation for the organization's growth and impact in Nepal.

üëë Role of the King in Establishment

The establishment of JCI Nepal was supported by the government and key leaders to promote youth leadership and community service across the country. While there isn't detailed public documentation on the specific involvement of the King of Nepal in the founding process, royal endorsement and patronage were crucial in legitimizing and supporting the organization's activities during its early years.

üèõÔ∏è JCI Tankisinwari

JCI Tankisinwari is a prominent local chapter of JCI Nepal, a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering young individuals through leadership development, community service, and personal growth. Established in Budhiganga Rural Municipality-1, Morang, JCI Tankisinwari has been actively contributing to the community by organizing various programs and initiatives aimed at fostering positive change.

The chapter's commitment to service and leadership is evident through its consistent participation in national events and its role in shaping the future leaders of Nepal. JCI Tankisinwari operates under the guidelines set by JCI Nepal, ensuring alignment with the organization's mission and values.

üë¶ JCI Tankisinwari Junior

JCI Tankisinwari Junior serves as the youth wing of JCI Tankisinwari, catering to individuals aged 12 to 18. The junior chapter focuses on nurturing leadership qualities, social responsibility, and personal development among young people in the region.

Established with the approval of the mother LOM (Local Organization Member) and ratified by its Annual General Meeting (AGM), JCI Tankisinwari Junior operates under the guidance and support of JCI Tankisinwari. The formation of the junior chapter was in line with the broader objective of JCI Nepal to involve youth in community service and leadership activities.

The junior chapter has been instrumental in organizing events such as the 4th Anniversary Celebration, which was a grand success, showcasing the talent and enthusiasm of its young members. Through such initiatives, JCI Tankisinwari Junior continues to inspire and empower the youth of Budhiganga, instilling in them the values of active citizenship and community service.
